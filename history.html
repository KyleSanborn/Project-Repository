2. "Potvin Sucks"

The "Potvin Sucks" chant is one that you are sure to hear every time you attend a Rangers game. While it did not originate during the playoffs, the story of the chant had major playoff implications. The chant was born during the 1979 season at Madison Square Garden when Ulf Nilsson, a young rangers player who was a key point contributor, took a vicious hit from Islander's captain Denis Potvin. After being hit into the boards, Nilsson's ankle broke in numerious places leaving him unable to play for the remainder of the season. A chant erupted inside of the Garden walls that the crowd repeated every time Potvin set foot on the ice throughout the game. During this era the Rangers were serious Stanley Cup contenders. After losing Nilsson, the Rangers were unable to make a run for the cup. To this day many Ranger fans still claim that they would have won the Stanely Cup that season had the injury not taken place. The chant has lived on inside the walls of Madison Square Garden since that day, and it will live there for as long as the Ranger's franchise exists.


3. The 1994 Stanely Cup

The Rangers were able to win their first Stanely Cup after 54 years in 1994. In their first cup appearance since 1979, the New York Rangers faced the Vancouver Canucks in a historical seven game series. Hall-of-famer Mark Messier scored the game winning goal in game seven of the series to cap of their chase for the cup. The Ranger's have not won a Stanely Cup since this season, which is why we are so excited about the possibility of the 28 year drought coming to an end in 2022!
